The Exact 30-Day Warmup Ramp

Picture this: a fresh domain is like a new neighbor moving in. You don't show up with a moving truck blasting music at midnight. Instead, you start small, wave hello, and build rapport over weeks. That's warmup in a nutshell. Tools like Instantly.ai lay out a precise schedule to convince ISPs you're legit, not a spammer.

It kicks off with just 2 emails per day for the first five days. You're sending to highly engaged contacts—people who've replied before—to rack up positive signals right away. The goal? A 1:1 ratio of sends to positive replies, which tells Gmail your emails are wanted. By days 6 to 15, you creep up to 10-25 sends daily, still chasing that reply sweet spot. Days 16 to 30 push 25-50, testing the waters before full production.

Why the slow build? ISPs watch delivery rates, opens, and replies like hawks. Skip this, and even perfect authentication won't save you from blocks. Reddit's cold email crowd swears by this ramp—agencies report jumping from zero to 95% inbox placement in a month.

Per-Inbox Caps and Rotation Triggers

Once warmed, no single inbox should exceed 40 emails a day. Gmail sniffs out bursts over 50 like a bloodhound, shoving 80-90% into spam. Smartlead.ai enforces this automatically, rotating to the next inbox after 40 sends or every few days. It's like tag-teaming sends so no one account looks suspicious.

Rotation isn't optional—it's the only way to hit 1,000+ daily without your root domain getting flagged at 500 sends flat.

Triggers fire based on volume, time, or engagement dips. Child domains—think sales2026.yourcompany.com—handle the load, each with their own authentication setup. Switch every 200-300 sends to spread risk and keep complaints under that critical 0.3% Yahoo threshold.

Key Insight

Building Your Rotation Pool

Start with 10-20 child domains under your root. Each gets independent authentication—SPF to list authorized senders, DKIM for message signatures, DMARC for policy enforcement. Connect them to unlimited inbox services in Instantly or Smartlead for seamless pooling.

Don't skimp here. Single-domain warriors hit IP limits fast; rotation isolates damage. If one subdomain draws complaints, retire it without torching your whole setup.

  • Align authentication records on every child domain; validate delivery before sending a single email.
  • Start warmup at 2 per day per inbox; check 98%+ delivery logs for the first five days.
  • Rotate at 40 sends daily; cap root domain under 500 total a day.
  • Maintain 1:1 reply ratio—pause scaling if it drops below 0.5:1 for three days.
  • Run weekly inbox placement tests aiming for 95% across providers.
  • Watch complaint rates under 0.3%; blacklist offenders immediately.
